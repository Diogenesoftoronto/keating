# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Keating serves self-directed learners, educators, developers, and researchers who want an AI tutor that can teach, assess, generate study artifacts, and improve its teaching policy over time. Users may arrive through the public web app, the CLI, or project documentation, and they need fast paths to provider setup, tutorials, release notes, and the actual chat experience.

## Product Purpose

Keating is a Pi-powered hyperteacher: a Socratic tutor and artifact generator for lesson plans, concept maps, quizzes, animations, verification, learner goals, and teaching-policy evolution. Success means learners can quickly configure a model, start a useful tutoring session, understand what changed across releases, and trust the system's local-first privacy and self-improvement boundaries.

## Brand Personality

Literary, rigorous, and practical. The interface can carry a retro terminal and paper-study-room tone, but it should never make task completion harder. The teaching posture is spirited and Socratic, while the product surface should stay legible, navigable, and dependable.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing pages that bury the usable product behind generic hero copy. Avoid changelogs that are just long walls of cards with no search or version filtering. Avoid documentation pages that force users to scroll from the top for every task. Avoid novelty navigation that duplicates links, hijacks scroll position, or turns standard controls into puzzles.

## Design Principles

1. Make the first useful action obvious.
2. Preserve user context, especially scroll position and reading position.
3. Keep the retro identity, but let standard navigation patterns do their job.
4. Let release history and documentation be searchable, filterable, and task-oriented.
5. Separate personality from protocol: voice can be expressive, controls must be predictable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Preserve keyboard access for navigation, search, filters, dialogs, and tabbed content. Respect reduced-motion preferences. Do not rely on color alone for state. Keep documentation and release notes scannable for users who arrive with a specific setup or troubleshooting task.
