Keating is a lantern for the age of answering machines.

It does not hand you the mountain. It teaches you to climb.

In its shell, questions become maps, lessons become artifacts, and uncertainty becomes a visible path.

Strong models gather around the learner like scaffolding, not a throne.

Keating exists for the moment after the answer arrives, when the real work begins: to test it, remake it, remember it, and speak it back in your own voice.

The future should not make students smaller.

Keating helps them become harder to replace.
